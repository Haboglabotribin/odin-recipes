San Choy Bow

What is San Choy Bow?

San Choy Bow is a Chinese dish that originated in the Sichuan province of China. It is a type of lettuce wrap that is typically filled with a mixture of ground meat (such as beef, pork, or chicken), vegetables (such as mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and water chestnuts), and a flavorful sauce. The filling is spooned onto a lettuce leaf, which is then wrapped around the mixture to form a bite-sized package. San Choy Bow is often served as an appetizer or a main dish, and is prized for its combination of flavors and textures. The dish is also a popular street food in many Asian countries, and has become popular in Western countries as well.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp peanut oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2cm piece ginger, fine grated
  • 500g lean pork mince, beef or chicken substitute optional
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 3 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 lime, juiced
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 cup breansprouts, trimmed
  • 12 large lettuce leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped roasted peanuts, optional

Steps:

  1. Heat wok or cast iron skillet over high heat until hot
  2. Add peanut oil and swirl to coat
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and pork.
  4. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes or until pork begins to change colour
  5. Add soy sauce and oyster sauce
  6. Add 2 teaspoons of lime juice and sesame oil
  7. Stir-fry for 3 minutes, adding bean sprouts throughout
  8. Spoon pork mixture into lettuce leaves
  9. Sprinkle with coriander and peanuts, if using
  10. Serve
